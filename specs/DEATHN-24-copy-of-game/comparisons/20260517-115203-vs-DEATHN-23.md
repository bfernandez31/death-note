## Implementation Choices Analysis

### [Spec] Red tint on the responsible zombie (FR-002)
| Ticket | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| DEATHN-24 | Stores `dying_zombie_index: ?usize` and re-finds slot by index in `drawZombies`. Adds distinct `BOSS_DARK_RED` for boss-triggered transitions. |
| DEATHN-23 | Stores `game_over_killer_zombie: ?*Zombie` pointer; reuses raylib.RED for both zombie and boss path. |

**Best**: DEATHN-24 — distinct tint for boss helps players understand which entity caused the loss; index-based reference avoids stale-pointer risk if the slot is freed during the transition.

### [Spec] Web persistence resilience (FR-011)
| Ticket | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| DEATHN-24 | Per-field `try/catch` JS wrapper, `Math.max(0, Math.floor(...))` sanitization, host-side clamping of `wave/wpm > maxInt(u32)` and `accuracy > 100` before `@intCast`. |
| DEATHN-23 | Custom `jsonGetU64` byte-scan parser; clamps via `@min(maxInt(u32), …)` only at intCast boundary. Falls through to zero on missing keys. |

**Best**: DEATHN-24 — defensive against corrupt JSON, not just missing keys. `@intCast` traps on out-of-range inputs, so DEATHN-23's clamp protects but DEATHN-24's wrapper is more robust.

### Decision Point 1: Native/Web split
| Ticket | Approach | Constitution | Trade-offs |
|--------|----------|--------------|------------|
| DEATHN-24 | Separate `loadHighScore`/`loadHighScoreWeb` and `saveHighScore`/`saveHighScoreWeb`; caller branches on `comptime is_web` | Yes — clear single-responsibility per fn | Slightly more code, easier to read |
| DEATHN-23 | Single `loadHighScore`/`saveHighScore` with `if (comptime …)` inside | Yes | More compact, harder to test individual branches |

**Best**: DEATHN-24 — easier to unit-test each platform independently.

### Decision Point 2: Same-frame race protection
| Ticket | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| DEATHN-24 | When `is_dying` triggers, short-circuits boss spawn, boss update, and wave-completion in the same frame (guarded by `if (!is_dying)`). |
| DEATHN-23 | Does not guard boss spawn or wave-completion against the new transition state. |

**Best**: DEATHN-24 — prevents a latent bug where a wave transition could start behind the dying overlay if the killing zombie was the last of the wave.

### Decision Point 3: Reset on restart
| Ticket | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| DEATHN-24 | Dedicated `resetSessionState()` helper covers `total_kills`, dying flags, and `is_new_high_score`. |
| DEATHN-23 | Inlines `total_kills = 0; is_new_highscore = false;` in the ENTER restart block; dying state isn't explicitly reset (relies on natural timer expiry before ENTER is reachable). |

**Best**: DEATHN-24 — single source of truth, fewer ways to forget a field on future additions.

### Decision Point 4: Magic-number discipline
| Ticket | Approach | Constitution III/3 |
|--------|----------|--------------------|
| DEATHN-24 | Adds 8 new named constants (`STATS_TITLE_Y`, `STATS_TITLE_SIZE`, `STATS_LINE_START_Y`, `STATS_LINE_SPACING`, `STATS_FONT_SIZE`, `STATS_RESTART_HINT_Y`, `STATS_RESTART_HINT_SIZE`, `DYING_DURATION`, `HIGHSCORE_DISK_SIZE`) | Yes — fully aligned |
| DEATHN-23 | Adds 4 constants (`GAME_OVER_TRANSITION_DURATION`, `HIGHSCORE_FILE`, `STATS_Y_START`, `STATS_Y_STEP`); leaves font sizes (48, 24, 18) and restart Y offset inlined | Partial |

**Best**: DEATHN-24 — more thorough adherence to constitution Code-Patterns rule #3 ("Explicit, named constants for tunables").

## Ranking & Recommendation

### 1. **DEATHN-24** — Best code (score: 93%)
**Why it wins**:
- Stronger constitution alignment: more named constants for layout tunables, paired-defer everywhere, `errdefer` patterns intact.
- Defensive web persistence: per-field JS try/catch and explicit `@intCast` clamps prevent traps on corrupt localStorage.
- Same-frame race guards on boss/wave logic prevent latent bugs.
- Distinct boss-dying tint improves UX cue for boss-caused losses.
- Cleaner restart path via `resetSessionState()` helper reduces future maintenance risk.

### 2. **DEATHN-23** — (score: 81%)
**Why it lost**:
- Fewer named constants — leaves font sizes and restart Y inlined, against constitution Code-Patterns rule #3.
- No same-frame guard between dying state and boss spawn / wave completion.
- Pointer-based killer reference is theoretically vulnerable if the slot is freed during the 1s transition (currently safe but fragile).
- Smaller and faster to build, but feature parity comes at the cost of robustness.
